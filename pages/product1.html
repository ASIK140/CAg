Silver Impregnated Graphite Contacts

Silver Impregnated Graphite (SIG) contacts are used in high safety applications of railway signaling, traffic signaling, and numerous other applications content that eliminates the risk of welding in the presence of graphite. SIG relay contacts combine the best properties of silver and graphite into a composite material. Distinctive features of the SIG material include its hard fusing characteristic, better conduction of heat and electricity, and easy brazing.This combination of properties makes these contacts suitable for high safety applications such as railway signaling, traffic signaling, and numerous other applications.

Silver Impregnated Graphite Heavy Duty Contacts

Silver impregnated graphite contacts for heavy-duty applications are engineered to withstand the rigors of high current loads, frequent switching cycles, and harsh operating conditions. These contacts feature enhanced conductivity due to silver impregnation, ensuring efficient electrical transmission even under heavy loads. Constructed with robust materials and techniques, they withstand mechanical stresses such as impact and vibration. Their high wear resistance, derived from silver impregnation, guarantees prolonged service life despite frequent switching. Moreover, they offer corrosion resistance, crucial for harsh environments with high humidity, dust, or chemical exposure. Engineered for temperature stability, they maintain performance across a wide range of conditions. With low contact resistance, they minimize energy loss and heat generation, vital for heavy-duty operations. Selection considerations include current rating, switching frequency, environmental factors, mechanical compatibility, adherence to reliability standards, and maintenance requirements.
